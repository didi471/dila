Tips for a Balanced Lifestyle

Nutrition



 Exercise



 Address Health Issues
  • Eat a balanced diet like vegetables, fruits, whole grains, lean proteins, and healthy fats
  • Aim for regular physical activity at least 150 minutes of moderate-intensity exercise or 75 minutes of vigorous-intensity exercise per week.
    		•
  • Get enough sleep at least 7-9 hours of sleep per night to help regulate hormones, metabolism, and overall health.
  • Watch portion sizes by paying attention to serving sizes and control your portions to maintain a healthy weight and prevent chronic diseases.
  • Stay active throughout the day likes try to incorporate physical activity into your daily routine, such as taking the stairs instead of the elevator or going for a short walk during lunch breaks.
  • Practice good hygiene by Wash your hands frequently, especially during cold and flu season, to prevent the spread of illness.
  • Hydrate adequately which is drink plenty of water throughout the day, aiming for at least 8 cups (64 oz) daily.
  • Incorporate strength training that include strength training exercises at least twice a week to build muscle and bone density.
    		•
  • Manage stress by practice stress-reducing techniques like meditation, deep breathing, or yoga to help manage stress.
    		•
  • Stay mindful of macronutrients to ensure you're getting enough protein, healthy fats, and complex carbohydrates to support overall health and energy levels.
  • Listen to your body with rest and recovery to make sure to give your body time to recover between workouts and take rest days as needed.
  • Stay on top of preventive care by do a regular health check-ups, screenings, and vaccinations can help prevent and detect health issues early on.
